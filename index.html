I am a python programmer and full-stack web developer. My love for computers and code began early in life with a playful challenge of loading my favorite computer game from an ancient 5.5" floppy disc. I was, of course, trying to load the DOS game, Chuck Yeager, flight simulator. My dad, who was in the air force at the time, taught me my first few commands and how to load the help files. I was mesmerized by the terminal's green flashing underscore. I loved the one to one input of the command line. I was using an i386 computer made 6 years earlier, the same year I was born.

Chuck Yeager came with a 1 inch thick printed game flight manual. Everything I needed to load the game, navigate the menu to select air-craft and mission, and procedures for take off and basic flight. I crashed a lot, the screen would blank white and the sad beep of the motherboard would chirp indicating you had crashed. I learned which keys adjusted flaps, throttle, and wheel brakes. Time was suspended for me when I was learning and when I was flying.

The thrill of flying in a simulated aircraft: I push it to render white vectors and polygons on a green screen. The runway strip stretches out in front of my f4-jet, ignition. The cooling fan of the i386 is whirring like a jet engine. No seatbelts, no safety nets, learn to fly or crash and burn!
