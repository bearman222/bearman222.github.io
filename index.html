I am a python programmer and full-stack web developer.

My love for computers and code began early in life with a playful challenge of loading my favorite computer game from an ancient 5.5" floppy disc. I was, of course, trying to load the DOS game, Chuck Yeager, andvanced flight simulator. My dad, who was in the air force at the time, taught me some commands and how to load the help files. I was mesmerized by the terminal's green flashing underscore. I was using an i386 computer made 6 years earlier, the same year I was born.

Chuck Yeager came with a 1 inch thick printed game flight manual. Everything I needed to load the game, navigate the menu to select air-craft and mission, and procedures for take off and basic flight. I crashed a lot, the screen would blank white and the sad beep of the motherboard would chirp indicating you had crashed. I learned which keys adjusted flaps, throttle, and wheel brakes. Time was suspended for me when I was learning and when I was flying.

The thrill of flying in a simulated aircraft:
The cooling fans inside my i386 PC whirr like jet engines.
Vectors and polygons render in 'real time' on a green and black CRT monitor.
The horizon fills the screen through the cockpit HUD of my F-4 jet.
Ignition! Thrust! Lift-off!
I was in control of the most powerful machine imaginable-
Jet streams directed by keyboard input-
There were no safety nets, learn to fly or crash and burn!

My obsession with computational machines would be life long.
